About Vieques

Vieques is a small island off the coast of mainland Puerto Rico. It's home to tons of empty beaches, wild horses and the world's brightest bioluminescent bay. You can find quite a bit of info about Vieques at enchanted-isle.com, but I'll give you the highlights.

Getting there

The best way to get to Vieques is by plane. You can take most commercial airlines into San Juan International airport. From there, you need to take a 20 minute prop plane flight to the island of Vieques. There are several companies that have a presence right in the San Juan Airport terminal. From my research, I've found Cape Air to be the cheap and reliable. There's also Seaborne Airlines, Vieques Airlink, and more.

In addition to flying, you can also take a cab from San Juan airport to Fajardo and take the ferry. The ferry is only $2 but the cab ride can be up to $80 so the cost ends up about the same. Once you get to Vieques you can use Publico, the public transportation system to get to your accomodations, or contact one of us. Publico is essentially a group taxi cab. There are several Publico drivers that you can call to come pick you up. The fare is usually around $3-$5. The Publico vans are kind of like the super shuttles to the airport. Where to Stay

There are two main towns on Vieques: Esperanza and Isabel II. The wedding, reception and the house we're renting are all located in Esperanza. The main thoroughfare in Esperanza is called the Malecon. On one side of the Malecon is the ocean and the other is restaurants and guesthouses. The house we'll be hosting parties at is two blocks from the Malecon. The wedding will be at Sun Bay, a 15 minute walk from the Malecon. And lastly, the reception will be held at Bili Restaurant, which is part of the Amapola Inn, on the Malecon. We've tried to plan it so that you won't need to rent a car if you don't want to. You could stay at a guesthouse in Esperanza and be within walking distance of all the activities. You could also use the Publico to get around to other places.

Here is a listing of guesthouses in Vieques, organized by region of the island. You can also check out TripAdvisor for reviews and more listings. We recommend staying in a place in Esperanza, as it's where all of the wedding events take place.
